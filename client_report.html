Client Report

Link to Site: https://violet-foxe.github.io/wdd130-hancock/index.html

How did you decide what content and images to use for your site plan?

Because this site was built to help skaters find skatespots across Rexburg, its functionality relied on photos of the spot being provided. So, I chose pictures featuring the different types of spots users would see on the site. As for the logo, I created it with AI tools to capture the appropriate rugged and industrial look.

How did you communicate with your developer? Was there any issues with communication?

Kaliess Bruger, my lead dev, excelled in communication and organization. She worked with me every step of the way by asking questions and providing a link to the site to see updated changes. We communicated both in person during class and over email.

Did your developer meet your expectations for your site?

Kaliess exceeded my expectations for the site. I worried that because my site plan was kind of niche, she wouldn't be able to catch the vision. However, Kaliess still nailed the rugged industrial skateboarding vibe while still keeping it polished.

What will you do differently next time you hire a team to do a project for you?

If I had to hire another team to do a project for me, I would spend more time on my wireframes. My wireframes were a little vague and unorganized, which may have caused my dev team to suffer.

Lead Developer Report

Link to Site: https://maximushancockimus.github.io/wdd130-chacon/

How did you manage your development team and comunicate the tasks for your junior devs?

I managed my developer team by optimizing my in-class time to talk with, plan, and delegate responsibilities to each of my junior developers. I triple checked with them to make sure they not only understood their assigned pages, but were well equipped to do so.

How did you communicate with your client? Was there any issues?

I communicated with my client Jaydyn Chacon on numerous occasions to nail down his MVP and clarifying anything I found on his site plan. There were no major issues, but one instance I can think of is when I was trying to link his logo to the site, the photo he provided was very low quality. So, I had to reach out to him in order to get a logo of higher quality.

How did you handle publishing your clients site and sharing it with the client?

I tried to keep an organized repository by keeping good pull request messages and frequently handling pull requests on Github Desktop. As soon as I had a published site on Github pages, I made sure he had access to the link so he could view the sites progress.

How was your experience with managing your site with GitHub?

Managing my site on Github had its ups and downs. I had to work with Brother Alvey to figure out why my site link wouldn't work when initially publishing my link. I learned that it was because my index file couldn't be within any file, because Github won't look inside any others. After some getting used to, I was able to manage my site with relative ease.

General Experience and Reflections

What was the most challenging part of this project for you? Were you able to overcome it?

The most challenging part of the project was designing my assigned pages according to someone else's eye. In my experience with web development so far, it has all been my ideas put to paper. However, this time I had to abide by rules and constraints placed by others.

What did you enjoy most about this project?

What I enjoyed most about this project may seem unusual. I actually enjoyed managing the repositories and learning about Github pages. Github is an essential skill in all of development and I was excited to learn how to better familiarize myself with it.

What would you do differently if you could do the project again?

If I had to do this project again, I would have spent more time on my own site plan. My site plan was a little rough and didn't provide very good instructions on the wireframes. In the future, I will be more considerate in providing clearer direction and vision with my site layout and design.

Do you feel more confident in working on real-world projects after this experience? Explain.

I feel more confident in working on real-world projects after this experience for a multitude of reasons. First, I have more experience working with a repository with multiple contributors. Second, I learned how to execute others ideas, not just my own. Third, I learned how to delegate responsibilities to others and how to take instructions from a superior.
